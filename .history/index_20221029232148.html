Frequently Asked Questions

Click to reveal answers!

What happens at a hackathon?

Participants (hackers) spend 36 hours working in small teams to design and build projects they're excited about. You bring your ideas and we give you everything you need to make them come to life, including workshops, mentors, food, and swag!

How many people are on a team?

Teams can range from 1 - 4 individuals.

What do teams usually make?

A wide variety of things! Web/mobile applications, AI/ML, AR/VR, games, even hardware. Whatever interests you most is likely to yield the best results!

Do I have to know how to code to attend?

Nope! We will provide resources including workshops and a mentorship system to help beginners get started. Additionally, one room will be dedicated to new hackers with several beginner project ideas.

Who runs TigerHacks?

TigerHacks is organized and hosted by a group of dedicated and passionate young nerds, who are members of the Mizzou Computing Association (MCA) at the University of Missouri.

Where do I register my team?

You can register using this link, starting on November 4th at 5PM.

What if I don't have a team?

If you need a team, you can find individuals in the Discord or DevPost

What can virtual participants view?

We plan to have a live feed and recording of the Opening Ceremony, Brainstorming Session, Tech Talks, Movie Night, How To Demo, and the Closing Ceremony.

The question I have isn't frequently asked!

If you've got something else on your mind, send it our way at muengrtigerhacks@missouri.edu! Additionally, feel free to message any member of the Planning Committee on Discord.
